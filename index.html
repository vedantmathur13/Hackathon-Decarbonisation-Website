Understanding The Problem

The Earth’s Climate Is Rapidly Changing And Is Projected To Continue To Change Over This Century And Beyond.

This change Depends Primarily On The Amount Of Greenhouse Gases Emitted Globally And Will Further Depend On How Earth’s Climate Will Further Respond To Those Emissions.

Rise In Global Average Temperature, and Sea Level, Melting of Glacier Ice etc. Provide Reliable Evidence Of A Warming Planet.








Decarbonization : Need Of The Hour

The Direct And Indirect Emissions Of Carbon Dioxide Including Other Greenhouse Gases, Have Played A Dominant Role In This Climate Change.

Currently, Our Energy Consumption Is The Primary Culprit Behind CO2 Emissions. As Per Research Data. Astonishingly, We Pump Over 35 Billion Metric Tons Of CO2 Into Our Atmosphere Annually.

If No Major Reductions Would Be There In These Emissions, The Increase In Annual Average Global Temperatures, Relative To Preindustrial Times, Could Reach 5°C Or More By The End Of This Century.

Decarbonization, The Way Out, Is The Strategic Reduction And Elimination Of Human-Induced Carbon Dioxide (CO2) Emissions From The Atmosphere, To Combat The Severe Impacts Of Global Warming. This Effort Demands A Worldwide Pivot Away From Fossil Fuels, Including Oil, Coal, And Gas.
